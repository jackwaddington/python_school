────────────────────────────────────────

Q1: Which of the following is an example of deductive reasoning?
  A) All swans are white because I've only seen white swans.
  B) If I study in a quiet environment, my learning improves.
  C) Some politicians are honest because John's favorite politician is honest.
  D) All men are mortal. Socrates is a man. Therefore, Socrates is mortal.
Answer: D

Q2: Which of the following arguments lacks a universal premise and commits the fallacy of hasty generalization?
  A) If a student believes that studying in a quiet environment improves their learning, they can test this hypothesis by observing their own study habits and performance in different environments.
  B) John claims that all politicians are dishonest because his favorite politician is dishonest.
  C) If I always wear blue shirts on Wednesdays and it rains on a Wednesday, then it rained because I wore a blue shirt.
  D) All cats have fur. Fluffy is a cat. Therefore, Fluffy has fur.
Answer: B
