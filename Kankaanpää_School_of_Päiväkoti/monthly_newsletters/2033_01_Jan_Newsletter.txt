 MONTHLY SUMMARY:
January was a vibrant and productive month at Kankaanpää School of Päiväkoti. With 539 active students and an impressive 632 graduates, our school continues to thrive as a beacon of excellence in early childhood education. Although we experienced a modest number of dropouts totaling 279, it is important to remember that every student's journey is unique, and we remain dedicated to supporting each one.

GRADUATE SPOTLIGHT:
This month, we proudly recognize the achievements of several remarkable students who demonstrated exceptional dedication and growth throughout their coursework. Among them are Mehak Singh (Child Development in Päiväkoti), Max Perez (Child Development in Päiväkoti), Mia Williams (Play-Based Learning Strategies), Ravi Patel (Play-Based Learning Strategies), Julio Ramirez (Infant and Toddler Care), Zara Yang (Päiväkoti Teacher Training), Yara Nakano (Päiväkoti Teacher Training), Elias Möller (Early Childhood Education), Sanaa El-Bakri (Early Childhood Education), and many more. Their success reflects our shared commitment to fostering a nurturing and enriching learning environment for all students.

LOOKING AHEAD:
As we embark on the month of February, we will continue our focus on providing exceptional education in Päiväkoti while remaining mindful of our school's capacity limitations. We invite new students to join us in exploring, discovering, and growing together. Additionally, we will be launching a new course, "Managing Challenging Behaviors in Päiväkoti," aimed at further equipping our educators with the tools they need to create supportive learning environments for all children. We look forward to another month of growth and development at Kankaanpää School of Päiväkoti.
