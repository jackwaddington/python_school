Q1: According to Attachment Theory, the bond between an infant and their caregiver is formed through repeated interactions and experiences of comfort, safety, and love. This bond provides the child with a secure base from which to explore the world and a safe haven to return to when frightened or overwhelmed. What does this describe?
  A) Egoism
  B) Egocentrism
  C) Attachment Theory
  D) Social Development
Answer: C

Q2: In Piaget's theory of cognitive development, a preschooler in the preoperational stage struggles to conserve quantity when presented with two identical glasses filled with water, then poured into one taller glass. Why does he struggle?
  A) Because he understands that the liquid is still the same quantity
  B) Because he is unable to mentally manipulate the quantity of the liquid
  C) Because he believes everyone sees the world from his own perspective
  D) Because he has not yet learned the concept of sharing
Answer: B
